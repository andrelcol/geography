Rock Types

Rocks are part of the lithosphere, the layer that makes up the Earth's solid surface. They are constituted by the union of different minerals, and are the different processes those responsible for the formation of the most varied types of rocks that are divided into 3 major types:

Magmatic: formed by the solidification of magma and divided into 2 groups according to the location of its solidification. Intrusives are those that solidify inside the earth's crust and extrusives are those that solidify outside the crust. The main difference between them is that in intrusive minerals group together and form crystals visible to the naked eye, as in most granites, while in extrusive ones it is not possible to observe this separation with the naked eye, an example is basalt. Metamorphic: they are also formed in the earth's crust. Very high pressure and temperature, strong friction, or the chemical combination of two or more minerals transform the structure of rocks already formed, such as marble, slate, quartz and gneiss. Sedimentary: formed outside the earth's crust by the weathering suffered by other rocks. Examples are sandstone and limestone.
